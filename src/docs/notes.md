# Notes

Reflections, ideas, honest self-talk. Things you want to remember or just need to say. Think of it as a running conversation with yourself — no structure required.

> ○ _Feeling scattered today. Too many tabs open in my brain. Need to just pick one thing and go._

### Why keep Notes?

The act of writing something down gets it out of your head and onto the page, where you can actually see it. It clears mental clutter, surfaces ideas you didn't know you had, and builds a habit of honest self-reflection.

### Pinning a note

If a note is something you want to keep close — a question you're sitting with, a reminder to yourself, a bit of writing you want to revisit — pin it. Pinned notes show up under **Pinned** in the sidebar so they stay one click away instead of getting buried further down in **All entries**.

Pin sparingly. The point is to surface a small handful of things you actually want in front of you, not to build another list.

### Try this

- Write whatever's on your mind — ideas, feelings, observations
- Let entries be messy, incomplete, or half-formed
- Come back and add another note if a thought continues

### Avoid this

- Editing or polishing before you've finished the thought, or after you've logged it
- Waiting until you have something "worth" writing down
- Using Notes as a project management or documentation tool
