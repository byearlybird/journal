# Tasks

Small actions you intend to get done today. If something doesn't happen, roll it over or let it go — no guilt either way. Tasks are about intent, not obligation.

> □ _Call the dentist_

### Why log Tasks?

Writing down what you intend to do today is a small act of commitment to yourself. It keeps you focused, reduces decision fatigue, and gives your day a shape — even if it changes.

### Carrying tasks forward

Anything you didn't finish on a prior day shows up under **Active** in the sidebar. It's a quiet reminder that those tasks are still there if you want them — not a backlog, not a streak counter. From there you can roll one into today, mark it complete, or cancel it if it no longer feels important.

### Try this

- Add tasks as they come to mind throughout the day
- Roll over anything that didn't get done
- Cancel tasks that no longer feel important — that's a valid outcome

### Avoid this

- Loading up your list with tasks that belong in a longer-term system. Focus on what you can do today
- Feeling guilty about unfinished tasks — priorities shift, and that's fine
